## Remaining Opportunities

These are areas where the copy could be further refined:

### 1. Hero subheadline — tighten the ending
Current ending "Removing friction for marketing teams so they can focus on growth" is softer than the core message's "without needing a data & analyst team behind it." Consider swapping to be more direct about not needing a data team.

### 2. Nav label "Results"
Original recommendation was to consider renaming to "Proof" or "Impact" — not yet changed. Low priority.

### 3. "Let's talk" CTA in header
Original recommendation was "See where you're wasting budget" or "Protect your ROAS" — currently "Let's talk" which is fine for a softer top-nav approach.

### 4. Feature impact badges
Could be stronger. For example, "Budget risks flagged before they cost you" could be more specific with a number or timeframe.

### 5. Trusted By section
Not assessed in original recommendations. Currently shows partner/integration logos (Adjust, Firebase, Snowplow, AppLovin, BigQuery, AWS, dbt, RevenueCat, AppsFlyer, Swift Games, Born). Consider adding a brief framing line like "Trusted by growth teams using:" or "Works with your stack:".

## v2 Additive Recommendations (Voss + 48 Laws of Power)

> **Philosophy:** Keep the existing 4 feature cards. Do NOT add more feature cards. Instead, use these additive sections to *imply* depth, create pull, and make the demo feel essential. The website should make visitors lean forward, not lean back satisfied.
>
> **Voss:** Information asymmetry creates pull. Show enough to label their pain precisely — hold back enough that the demo is where the "that's right" moment happens.
>
> **48 Laws — Law 3 (Conceal your intentions), Law 16 (Use absence to increase honor), Law 4 (Say less than necessary):** A company that shows 4 features and implies more feels larger than one that lists 9 features and that's everything.

### Additive Section 1: Feature card copy upgrades (fold missing capabilities into existing cards)

Do not add new feature cards. Instead, upgrade the *body text* of existing cards to imply Deep Search, Explain Mode, Forecasting, Alerts, and Slack — without dedicated sections.

**Daily Digest — add to body:**
> Current ending: "...delivered before your first meeting."
> New ending: "...delivered before your first meeting — in your dashboard or Slack."

*Why:* Slack integration is implied, not showcased. Creates a "wait, it's in Slack too?" moment during the demo. (Law 16 — absence increases value.)

**AI Chat — upgrade body:**
> Current: "...respond with context, charts, and recommended next steps."
> New: "...respond with context, charts, and recommended next steps. For complex questions, Lupe runs multi-step investigations automatically — breaking the problem into analytical steps, comparing time windows, and synthesizing findings."

*Why:* Deep Search becomes an *aspect* of AI Chat, not a separate feature. This implies depth without adding a card. The visitor thinks "wait, it can do multi-step analysis?" — that's a demo question. (Voss — create calibrated curiosity.)

**AI Chat — upgrade impact badge:**
> Current: "From question to action in seconds, not days"
> New: "From question to root cause in seconds, not days"

*Why:* "Root cause" implies Explain Mode without naming it. It signals the AI doesn't just retrieve — it diagnoses. (Law 4 — say less, imply more.)

**Company Overview — add to body:**
> Current ending: "...without writing a line of code or filing a ticket with your data team."
> New ending: "...without writing a line of code or filing a ticket with your data team. Predictive ROAS and LTV forecasts update daily as new data flows in."

*Why:* Forecasting becomes part of the command center, not a standalone promise. This is more credible — it's a feature *within* a product, not a bullet point on a slide. (Law 3 — concealed within something larger.)

**Daily Digest — upgrade impact badge:**
> Current: "Budget risks flagged before they cost you"
> New: "Risks flagged. Causes explained. Actions recommended."

*Why:* Three-beat rhythm implies a three-layer product (detect → explain → recommend) without spelling it out. Implies Explain Mode and Alerts together. (Voss — tactical empathy; the prospect's real fear is "I see the red number but don't know why.")

### Additive Section 2: "Not just dashboards" one-liner (NEW — place between Features and How It Works)

- **Copy:** "Dashboards show you what happened. Lupe tells you why — and what to do next."
- **Format:** Single line, centered, slightly larger font. No heading. No explanation.

*Why this works:*
- **Voss — labeling:** This labels every BI tool and dashboard the visitor has ever used as insufficient. It's a mirror: "it seems like your current tools show you *what* but not *why*." That triggers a "that's right" reaction.
- **Law 4 — say less than necessary:** One sentence does more than a feature card. It repositions every competitor without naming them.
- **For VCs scanning:** This line is the moat statement. It draws the line between "analytics tool" and "intelligence layer." A VC reads this and immediately understands the category.

### Additive Section 3: "Built different" strip (NEW — place after How It Works, before Results)

A horizontal strip with 4 short items. Not a full section — a confidence signal.

| Icon | Label | One-liner |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 🔒 | Your warehouse | Your data never leaves your infrastructure. We query it in place. |
| 🧠 | Schema-driven | Adapts to your data model automatically. No hardcoded logic. |
| ⚡ | Semantic layer | One definition of every metric. Consistent everywhere. |
| 🔌 | Works with your stack | Ad networks, MMPs, warehouses, revenue tools. Plug in, don't rip out. |

> **Do NOT use emojis in production.** Use simple icons or just text. The emojis above are placeholders for the design team.

*Why this works:*
- **For customers:** Trust signals. "My data stays with me" is the #1 concern for enterprise buyers.
- **For VCs (Law 3):** "Schema-driven" and "semantic layer" are technical depth markers. A VC's technical partner reads this and thinks "this is real engineering, not a GPT wrapper." But it's said casually — as a strip, not a dedicated section. Confidence, not persuasion. (Law 4 — the most powerful signals are the ones you don't emphasize.)
- **Voss:** This is an accusation audit in disguise. It preemptively answers: "Will my data be safe?" "Will it break when we change our models?" "Do we have to rip out our stack?" — without the visitor having to ask.

### Additive Section 5: Results section refinements

**Heading:**
> Current: "Results that speak for themselves"
> New: "Results from early customers"

*Why:* "Speak for themselves" is a cliché that actually undermines confidence. "Early customers" implies traction — plural customers, active usage. For a VC, this is the difference between "they have a demo" and "they have revenue." (Law 3 — imply traction without stating ARR.)

**Disclaimer:**
> Current: "Based on pilot results"
> New: "Based on design partner results"

*Why:* "Pilot" sounds like testing. "Design partner" is standard SaaS language for active, collaborative customers who are co-building with you. VCs know this term. (Voss — labels matter. The same data, different label, different perception.)

### What's NOT on the website (by design)

These capabilities exist in the product but are deliberately withheld from the website to create demo pull:

| Held back | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| Deep Search (multi-step investigations) | Shown *inside* AI Chat copy. Full demo of this is a "wow" moment — don't give it away. |
| Explain Mode (driver analysis) | Implied by "root cause" and "explain why" language. Demo it live. |
| Proactive Alerts | Mentioned in Before/After and Slack FAQ. Not a card — alerts are a retention feature, not an acquisition feature. |
| Slack as a full platform | Implied in Daily Digest and FAQ. The live Slack demo sells itself — don't pre-sell it. |
| Multi-model AI routing | In "Built different" strip as "schema-driven." Don't explain architecture on a SaaS homepage. |
| Collections / saved views | Too in-product. Not relevant to first-visit interest. |
| MoM/WoW/YoY comparisons | Table-stakes once you're a user. Not a homepage headline. |

> **The principle:** Every feature you hold back is a reason for the prospect to take the demo call. The website's job is not to be complete — it's to make the demo feel essential. (Voss: "No deal is better than a bad deal" — no feature card is better than one that satisfies curiosity before the call.)
